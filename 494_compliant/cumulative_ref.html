In my semesters at Iowa State University, I’ve learned lots of very important skills about how to be an engineer. All my different classes have taught me both technical and soft skills that I’ve gotten to use in internships, and hopefully in a full-time job. Classes like Software Development Practices (COM S 309), Computer Graphics Processing and Architecture (CPRE 480), and Senior Design (CPRE 491) have not only taught me the corresponding technical skills, but also how to work with a team on a technical project. While the pandemic was generally not a good thing, having to learn remotely meant that myself and other engineering students were forced to find ways to work and learn while remote. Tools like Discord and VSCode’s Live Share extension made communication and collaborative working manageable in the semesters we spent apart. Even now that we’re back to mostly in-person classes, those same tools improve how we work.

Through all the general education electives I’ve taken at Iowa State, I’ve learned about topics beyond those taught in my engineering courses, and these classes have taught me different ways of looking at problems, and how problem solving can go beyond your standard technical courses.

Some of my most enjoyable gen ed classes have been HSP M 383, 383L, and 385x. While I initially took these classes because I wanted to have a fun time trying different beverages, I actually learned a lot about the history of wine, beer, spirits, and mixed drinks. The historical development of lots of these drinks can be interesting stories of innovation and ingenuity. One such story is the creation of America’s first beer style, the California Common (sometimes called Steam Beer). When the gold rush began in 1849, thousands moved out to California in hopes to strike it rich, and even more people moved out to support the miners and make some money themselves. When some brewers went west, they brought with them yeast strains commonly used for making lagers, which ferment at refrigeration level temps. Without any sort of refrigeration, brewers improvised, using the lager yeast at warmer temperatures, and creating a new style of beer that has persisted to this day.
